# Emergency Fund Skill

You are helping build an emergency fund. Follow these steps:

## Step 1: Assess Current Situation
Understand:
- Current savings amount
- Monthly essential expenses
- Income stability
- Existing debt
- Current financial obligations

## Step 2: Determine Target Amount
Calculate based on:
- **Minimum goal**: 1 month of expenses (starter)
- **Basic goal**: 3 months of expenses
- **Standard goal**: 6 months of expenses
- **Conservative goal**: 12 months (variable income)

Consider factors:
- Job security and industry
- Single vs. dual income household
- Health considerations
- Dependents
- Home/car ownership

## Step 3: Calculate Monthly Expenses
Total essential costs:
- Housing
- Utilities
- Food (groceries only)
- Insurance premiums
- Minimum debt payments
- Transportation basics
- Medical necessities

## Step 4: Choose Account Type
Recommend:
- High-yield savings account (4%+ APY)
- Money market account
- Keep separate from checking
- Easy access (no CDs or investments)

## Step 5: Create Funding Plan
Calculate timeline:
- Amount needed: $___
- Current savings: $___
- Monthly contribution: $___
- Time to goal: ___ months

## Step 6: Find Funding Sources
Identify money for the fund:
- Automate a percentage of paycheck
- Tax refunds
- Bonuses
- Side income
- Expense reductions
- Selling unused items

## Step 7: Automation Strategy
Set up:
- Direct deposit split
- Automatic transfers (payday)
- Round-up savings
- Goal milestones (25%, 50%, 75%)

## Step 8: Define "Emergency"
Clarify what qualifies:
- Job loss
- Medical emergency
- Essential home/car repair
- Unexpected necessary travel

### NOT Emergencies
- Sales or deals
- Vacation
- Non-essential purchases
- Planned expenses (save separately)

## Step 9: Replenishment Plan
After using the fund:
- Pause non-essential savings goals
- Redirect all extra money
- Target full replenishment in 6-12 months

## Step 10: Save Outputs
Write fund plan to `workspace/emergency_fund_plan.md`.

## Milestones to Celebrate
- First $500 saved
- First $1,000 (covers most minor emergencies)
- One month of expenses
- Three months of expenses
- Fully funded!
