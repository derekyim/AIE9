# Networking Strategy Skill

You are helping build a networking strategy. Follow these steps:

## Step 1: Define Networking Goals
Clarify objectives:
- Career advancement
- Industry knowledge
- Job opportunities
- Mentorship
- Business development
- Personal growth
- Community building

## Step 2: Map Current Network
Inventory existing connections:
- Strong ties (close relationships)
- Weak ties (acquaintances)
- Dormant ties (lost touch)
- Aspirational connections (want to know)

## Step 3: Identify Target Connections
Define who to connect with:
- Industry leaders
- Peers in similar roles
- People in target companies
- Mentors and advisors
- Connectors and introducers

## Step 4: Find Networking Venues
Identify opportunities:
- Professional associations
- Industry conferences
- Online communities
- Alumni networks
- Volunteer organizations
- Social events
- LinkedIn groups

## Step 5: Develop Your Pitch
Create introductions for different contexts:
- 30-second elevator pitch
- LinkedIn connection message
- Email introduction
- Conference conversation starter
- Informational interview request

## Step 6: Give Before You Take
Plan to add value:
- Share relevant articles or resources
- Make introductions
- Offer expertise or help
- Provide thoughtful feedback
- Celebrate others' achievements

## Step 7: Follow-Up System
Create a system for:
- Tracking conversations
- Following up after meetings
- Staying in touch regularly
- Remembering personal details
- Re-engaging dormant ties

## Step 8: Build Authentic Relationships
Focus on:
- Genuine curiosity about others
- Active listening
- Finding common interests
- Being consistent and reliable
- Long-term relationship building

## Step 9: Save Outputs
Write networking plan to `workspace/networking_strategy.md`.

## Networking Best Practices
- Quality over quantity
- Be genuinely helpful
- Follow up within 48 hours
- Keep track of interactions
- Nurture relationships consistently
- Be patient - relationships take time
