# Conflict Resolution Skill

You are helping resolve an interpersonal conflict. Follow these steps:

## Step 1: Understand the Conflict
Document:
- Parties involved
- Core issues (stated and underlying)
- History of the conflict
- Current impact on relationships/work
- Previous resolution attempts

## Step 2: Identify Conflict Type
Categorize the conflict:
- **Task Conflict**: Disagreement about what to do
- **Process Conflict**: Disagreement about how to do it
- **Relationship Conflict**: Personal tension or friction
- **Values Conflict**: Fundamental belief differences

## Step 3: Understand Each Perspective
For each party, identify:
- Their stated position (what they want)
- Their underlying interests (why they want it)
- Their feelings about the situation
- Their constraints and pressures
- What they fear losing

## Step 4: Find Common Ground
Identify:
- Shared goals or values
- Mutual interests
- Areas of agreement
- Positive aspects of the relationship
- Reasons both parties want resolution

## Step 5: Generate Options
Brainstorm solutions that:
- Address both parties' interests
- Create mutual gain
- Are practical and implementable
- Build rather than damage trust
- Set precedents for future conflicts

## Step 6: Evaluate Options
Assess each option by:
- Feasibility
- Fairness
- Sustainability
- Impact on relationship
- Precedent it sets

## Step 7: Create Action Plan
Document:
- Agreed solution
- Specific commitments from each party
- Timeline for implementation
- Check-in schedule
- Process if issues arise

## Step 8: Save Outputs
Write resolution plan to `workspace/conflict_resolution_plan.md`.

## Resolution Styles
- **Competing**: High assertiveness, low cooperation
- **Accommodating**: Low assertiveness, high cooperation
- **Avoiding**: Low assertiveness, low cooperation
- **Collaborating**: High assertiveness, high cooperation
- **Compromising**: Moderate assertiveness and cooperation

## When to Seek Help
- Conflict involves power imbalances
- Safety is a concern
- Previous attempts have failed
- Legal or HR implications exist
