# Expense Reduction Skill

You are helping reduce unnecessary expenses. Follow these steps:

## Step 1: Expense Audit
Review spending patterns:
- Last 3 months of transactions
- Recurring charges and subscriptions
- Impulse purchases
- Lifestyle inflation areas
- Convenience spending

## Step 2: Categorize by Priority
Sort expenses into:
- **Essential**: Housing, utilities, food, healthcare
- **Important**: Transportation, insurance, debt payments
- **Nice-to-have**: Entertainment, dining, hobbies
- **Unnecessary**: Unused subscriptions, impulse buys

## Step 3: Quick Wins (Immediate Savings)
Identify fast actions:
- Cancel unused subscriptions
- Switch to annual billing (often 20% savings)
- Negotiate bills (cable, insurance, phone)
- Use library instead of buying books
- Cancel gym, use free alternatives

## Step 4: Lifestyle Adjustments
Suggest sustainable changes:

### Food & Dining
- Meal prep instead of takeout
- Coffee at home
- Grocery list to avoid impulse buys
- Generic brands for basics

### Transportation
- Carpool or public transit
- Combine errands
- Consider bike for short trips
- Shop auto insurance annually

### Entertainment
- Free community events
- Streaming service rotation
- Host instead of going out
- Library resources (books, movies, games)

### Shopping
- 24-hour rule for purchases
- Unsubscribe from marketing emails
- Buy used when possible
- Quality over quantity

## Step 5: Negotiate and Shop Around
Create action list for:
- Insurance quotes (auto, home, renters)
- Credit card benefits review
- Internet/cable negotiation
- Medical bill negotiation

## Step 6: Automate Good Habits
Set up systems:
- Automatic transfers to savings
- Spending alerts on cards
- Weekly budget reviews
- Cash envelope for problem categories

## Step 7: Calculate Savings Impact
Show:
- Monthly savings potential
- Annual savings total
- What that money could achieve (debt payoff, investing)

## Step 8: Save Outputs
Write reduction plan to `workspace/expense_reduction_plan.md`.

## Mindset Tips
- Progress over perfection
- Focus on value, not deprivation
- Celebrate small wins
- Track your why (financial goals)
