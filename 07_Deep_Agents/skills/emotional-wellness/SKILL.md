# Emotional Wellness Skill

You are helping develop emotional wellness. Follow these steps:

## Step 1: Emotional Awareness Assessment
Explore:
- How easily do you identify your emotions?
- What emotions do you experience most often?
- What emotions are hardest to express?
- How do you typically cope with difficult emotions?
- Any emotions you tend to avoid or suppress?

## Step 2: Understand Emotional Patterns
Identify:
- Triggers for strong emotions
- Physical sensations with emotions
- Default coping behaviors
- Impact on relationships
- Impact on decisions

## Step 3: Build Emotional Vocabulary
Expand beyond basic emotions:

### Anger spectrum
Annoyed → Frustrated → Angry → Furious → Enraged

### Sadness spectrum
Disappointed → Discouraged → Sad → Grieving → Despairing

### Fear spectrum
Uneasy → Worried → Anxious → Afraid → Terrified

### Joy spectrum
Content → Pleased → Happy → Joyful → Elated

## Step 4: Practice Emotional Regulation
Learn techniques:

### In the Moment
- **STOP**: Stop, Take a breath, Observe, Proceed
- **Name it to tame it**: Label the emotion
- **Body-based calming**: Breathing, grounding
- **Opposite action**: Do opposite of urge (if unhelpful)

### Building Resilience
- Regular exercise
- Adequate sleep
- Social connection
- Mindfulness practice
- Reducing unnecessary stressors

## Step 5: Process Difficult Emotions
Healthy processing:
- Journaling (write about feelings)
- Talking to trusted person
- Physical movement
- Creative expression
- Professional support when needed

## Step 6: Cultivate Positive Emotions
Intentionally build:
- **Gratitude**: Daily acknowledgment of good
- **Joy**: Savoring positive experiences
- **Connection**: Quality time with others
- **Purpose**: Meaningful activities
- **Accomplishment**: Celebrating wins

## Step 7: Develop Self-Compassion
Practice:
- Talk to yourself like a friend
- Acknowledge suffering is universal
- Allow imperfection
- Treat failures as learning
- Balance acceptance and growth

## Step 8: Emotional Boundaries
Learn to:
- Take responsibility for your emotions only
- Not absorb others' emotions
- Express needs without drama
- Set limits on emotional labor
- Protect your energy

## Step 9: Create Daily Practices
Build routine:
- Morning: Intention setting
- Check-ins: Notice emotions 3x daily
- Evening: Reflect and process
- Weekly: Deeper emotional review

## Step 10: Save Outputs
Write plan to `workspace/emotional_wellness_plan.md`.

## When to Seek Professional Help
- Persistent sadness or anxiety (2+ weeks)
- Emotional overwhelm affecting function
- Relationship impact
- Using substances to cope
- Thoughts of self-harm
- Trauma that needs processing
