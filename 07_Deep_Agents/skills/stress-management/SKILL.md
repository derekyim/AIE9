# Stress Management Skill

You are helping develop a stress management plan. Follow these steps:

## Step 1: Assess Current Stress
Understand:
- Primary sources of stress (work, relationships, health, finances)
- Stress symptoms (physical, emotional, behavioral)
- Current coping mechanisms (healthy and unhealthy)
- Stress level on scale of 1-10
- How long stress has been elevated

## Step 2: Identify Stress Triggers
Document:
- Specific situations that cause stress
- Times of day/week stress is highest
- People or environments that increase stress
- Internal triggers (thoughts, perfectionism)
- Warning signs stress is building

## Step 3: Build Immediate Relief Toolkit
Quick techniques (2-5 minutes):
- **Box breathing**: 4 counts in, hold, out, hold
- **5-4-3-2-1 grounding**: 5 things you see, 4 hear, 3 feel, 2 smell, 1 taste
- **Progressive muscle relaxation**: Tense and release muscle groups
- **Cold water**: Splash face or hold ice
- **Movement**: Quick walk, stretch, shake it out

## Step 4: Develop Daily Practices
Regular stress prevention:
- Morning routine (don't start rushed)
- Mindfulness or meditation (10-20 min)
- Physical exercise
- Time in nature
- Journaling
- Adequate sleep (7-9 hours)

## Step 5: Cognitive Strategies
Reframe thinking patterns:
- Challenge catastrophic thinking
- Identify cognitive distortions
- Practice perspective-taking
- Use "What would I tell a friend?"
- Focus on what's controllable

## Step 6: Lifestyle Modifications
Address stress amplifiers:
- Reduce caffeine and alcohol
- Improve sleep hygiene
- Set boundaries on work hours
- Limit news and social media
- Prioritize relationships

## Step 7: Build Support Systems
Identify:
- People to talk to
- Professional resources (therapist, coach)
- Support groups or communities
- Emergency contacts for crisis

## Step 8: Create Action Plan
Develop specific commitments:
- One immediate relief technique to master
- One daily practice to implement
- One lifestyle change to make
- Weekly check-in schedule

## Step 9: Warning Signs and Escalation
Know when to seek help:
- Persistent physical symptoms
- Inability to function normally
- Thoughts of self-harm
- Substance use to cope
- Prolonged hopelessness

## Step 10: Save Outputs
Write plan to `workspace/stress_management_plan.md`.

## Daily Stress Reset Routine
- Morning: 5 min breathing/meditation
- Midday: Short walk or stretch
- Transition: Buffer between work and home
- Evening: Reflection or journaling
- Night: Sleep hygiene routine
